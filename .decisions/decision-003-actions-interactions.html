Actions & Interactions

Interaction

How do the three key actions work? Play Setlist starts a queue of songs the artist is likely to play. Get Tickets opens Ticketmaster. Attendance marks whether you're going. The question is where these live and how they interact with the collapsed/expanded states.

Recommended. The collapsed card is clean — just info, no buttons. All actions live in the expanded Hero Reveal. Attendance and other secondary actions are in the long-press context menu (already implemented). This keeps the collapsed view maximally scannable and the expanded view action-focused.

Works well when

  • Cleanest collapsed view — pure info
  • Clear mental model: tap to see details + act
  • Context menu handles edge actions without clutter

Watch out for

  • Can't quick-mark attendance from list view

Same as Option A but with one addition: a small attendance toggle icon on the right edge of the collapsed Poster Strip. Tap it to quickly mark "going" without expanding. The rest of the card still expands on tap. Balances clean design with the most common quick action.

Works well when

  • Quickly mark shows from the list
  • Only one small icon — doesn't clutter
  • Most intuitive for "I'm scrolling, let me mark this"

Watch out for

  • Small tap target on the collapsed card
  • Accidental taps (attendance vs expand)

Option C
Chosen

Swipe Actions

1See clean Poster Strip (no icons)
↓
2Swipe left → reveal "Tickets" + "Setlist" behind
↓
3Swipe right → reveal "Going" toggle behind
↓
4Tap card → Hero Reveal with all details
iOS-style swipe gestures on the collapsed card. Swipe left reveals action buttons (Tickets, Setlist). Swipe right reveals attendance toggle. Tap still opens the expanded view. The collapsed card stays perfectly clean — all actions are hidden behind gestures. Power-user friendly.

Works well when

  • Cleanest possible collapsed view
  • All actions accessible without expanding
  • Familiar iOS/Android pattern

Watch out for

  • Hidden gestures — discoverability problem
  • Complex to implement well (spring physics, snap points)
  • Conflicts with page scroll on mobile

All Visible

1Poster Strip + Play, Tickets, Attendance icons (right)
↓
2Tap any icon → immediate action
↓

All three action icons visible on the collapsed card (Play, Tickets, Attendance) on the right side. Zero taps to act — everything is immediately accessible. The trade-off is a busier collapsed view with 3 icons competing with the date block for space on the right.

Works well when

  • Zero friction for any action
  • Power users who know what they want
  • Desktop/tablet where space is abundant

Watch out for

  • Collapsed card feels busy with 3 icons
  • Small tap targets on mobile
  • Less room for artist/venue text
