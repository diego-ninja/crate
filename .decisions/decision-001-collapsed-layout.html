Collapsed Show Card Layout

Visual

This is what users see 90% of the time — the collapsed card in a list of upcoming shows. It needs to communicate artist, venue, date, and city at a glance without feeling cluttered. The card should invite tapping to expand. Think of it as the "headline" of a show.

Chosen

Poster Strip

Converge

w/ Coalesce, Nails
MAY 23 FRI
The artist photo takes the left edge like a poster or vinyl spine. Info fills the center, date block sits right. Has a visual anchor (the photo) that makes the list feel rich without being heavy. The photo doubles as a tap target that hints "there's more here".

Works well when

  • Artists have good photos (most do)
  • Scanning a long list — photo gives instant recognition
  • Mobile — natural thumb reach for all zones

Watch out for

  • Artists without photos get a blank square
  • Image loading adds weight to the list

Recommended. Evokes a real ticket stub: date block on the left separated by a dashed "tear" line, info in the center. No images to load, pure typography. The dashed border gives it character without adding weight. A subtle chevron hints expandability. Feels native to a music app.

Works well when

  • Fast rendering — no images, pure CSS
  • Strong visual identity from the "stub" metaphor
  • Works equally well for any artist

Watch out for

  • Less visual richness than photo-based options
  • Date block takes horizontal space on narrow screens

Option C
Chosen

Gradient Bar

Converge Fri May 23
Razzmatazz · Barcelona
w/ Coalesce, Nails
✓
Minimal and elegant. A thin gradient accent line on the left gives each card color identity. Date is inline with the artist name. No separate date block — everything flows as text. The gradient background adds warmth without images. Attendance toggle is a small icon on the right.

Works well when

  • Maximum density — smallest card height
  • Clean, modern aesthetic
  • Genre-colored accent line adds personality

Watch out for

  • Date is less scannable inline vs. in a block
  • Can feel too minimal for some tastes

Option D
Chosen

Banner Card

FRI MAY 23
Converge
Razzmatazz · Barcelona · w/ Coalesce, Nails
A mini banner at the top (artist background image with gradient fade) gives visual impact without taking much space. Date overlays the banner. Below it, artist name and venue in a compact text block. Feels like a mini event poster. More visual than the other options but taller.

Works well when

  • Artists have background images — looks stunning
  • Strong visual presence in the feed
  • Date on the banner is easy to scan

Watch out for

  • Taller than other options — fewer cards visible
  • Image loading and missing-image fallback needed
  • Two zones (banner + text) can feel heavy
